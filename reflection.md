# Reflection on Using AI Tools for Portfolio Development

Building this portfolio website with Quarto was my first real experience using AI tools for a complete project, and it taught me a lot about both the benefits and limitations of these technologies.

## Getting Started with AI

When I first started thinking about how to structure a portfolio website, I honestly wasn't sure where to begin. I used ChatGPT to help brainstorm what pages I should include and what kind of content would make sense for each section. The AI suggested a five-page layout (Home, Resume, Projects, Skills, Contact) which seemed pretty standard for portfolios, so I went with that.

The AI was helpful in giving me a starting framework. It suggested what should go on each page - like putting highlights on the home page and organizing my resume in a traditional format. This saved me time because I didn't have to research portfolio best practices from scratch.

## Working with AI-Generated Content

Once I had the structure, I asked the AI to help draft content for each page. This is where things got interesting. The AI created initial drafts that were... okay, but very generic. For example, when describing my work at Appsyco, it used really formal language and made everything sound more impressive than it actually was.

I had to go through and rewrite a lot of it to sound like me and to be accurate about what I actually did. I added specific details about the forecasting projects I worked on and the dividend analysis tasks. The AI couldn't know these details - only I could provide them.

The tone was another issue. The AI writes in this very polished, professional way that doesn't really sound like how I would naturally explain my experience. I spent time making it more conversational while still keeping it professional enough for a portfolio.

## What Worked Well

The biggest advantage was speed. The AI generated a complete website structure and initial content in maybe 15-20 minutes. If I had started from scratch, it probably would have taken me several hours just to figure out the layout and write first drafts.

The AI was also really good at technical stuff like creating the CSS styling and making sure all the Markdown formatting was correct. I don't have much web development experience, so having the AI handle the styling saved me from having to learn CSS in depth.

## Where AI Fell Short

The main limitation was that AI can't actually know anything about my personal experiences. Every specific detail about my education, my projects, and my work had to come from me. The AI could create a template, but I had to fill in the real substance.

Another challenge was that the AI couldn't help me decide what to emphasize or how to position myself for the kind of jobs I want. Those strategic decisions required me to think about my career goals and what employers in analytics are looking for.

## What I Learned

This project showed me that AI tools are really useful for getting started and handling technical tasks, but they can't replace the human element. The final portfolio needed my personal touch, my actual experiences, and my judgment about what matters.

I think the best approach is using AI as a starting point and then putting in the work to make it authentic and personal. The AI helped me build something faster than I could have alone, but the value comes from the parts that are genuinely mine.
